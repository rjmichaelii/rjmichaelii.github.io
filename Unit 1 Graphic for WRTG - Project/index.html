Alaska Wildlife Conservation Center

Wood Bison Restoration

On the left wood bison are pictured as they are being transported to their new home in Shageluk Alaska. The process started by loading 100 wood bison into special container, then transported to Anchorage, Alaska and finally loaded on a C130 plane and flown to the Innoko valley. Here they are now roaming freely for the first time in 100 years.

Goals

❮ ❯
These images are plans to show what the new Matson Ocean Education Center will look like. It will include a square classroom and a large deck. The class room is large enough to hold 30-50 people and comes with restroom facilites, storage from shipping containers, solar panels, and a rain collectino system. Here instructors will focus on educational programs involving the Cook Inlet beluga whales, icebergs, and tides. Other items incldue signs, a life-size beluga skeleton, binoculars, Secchi disks (for measuring water turbidity), and a hydrophone for listening to underwater sounds.
These three images are plans to show the new Animal Clinic and Housing Building will look like. On the first floor they plan to create a more efficient kitchen for diet preparation. Next a improved animal infoirmary with a garage door for larger animals, hydraulic table/lift systems for surgeries, another wing for biosecurity, and a workstation for the head veterinarian. Last for the first floor, storage for gear and supplies, keeper workstations, restrooms, and a breakroom. For the second floor this is where they will have additional housing to accommodate 16 people with multiple restrooms, showers, laundry facilities, a big kitchen, multiple sinks, and internet connection.
